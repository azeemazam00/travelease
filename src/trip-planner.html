Trip Planning Guide

How to Travel

  • 🚌 Long-distance buses available from major cities (Daewoo, Faisal Movers).
  • 🚙 Local jeeps required for remote areas like Fairy Meadows or Deosai.
  • 👨‍✈️ Hire local guides for treks, especially in Skardu & Naran.

Where to Stay

  • 🏨 Hotels in cities like Hunza, Skardu, and Gilgit.
  • 🏠 Guest houses and home stays for authentic local experience.
  • 📱 Use sites like Booking.com or local apps like GoGhoom.

Budgeting Tips

  • 💰 Estimate Rs. 4000–7000 per day for mid-range travel.
  • 🛵 Use shared transport to save money.
  • 🍜 Eat at local dhabas instead of tourist cafes.

Packing Essentials

  • 🧥 Warm clothing, even in summer (mountains are cold at night).
  • 🥾 Hiking boots & rain jackets.
  • 🧴 Sunscreen, water bottles, medicines, power bank.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is Wi-Fi available in the north?
Yes, most hotels and cafes in cities have Wi-Fi, but connectivity is weak in remote valleys.

Do I need a guide?
For remote trekking routes like Nanga Parbat base camp or Skardu’s hidden lakes — yes.

Is it safe to travel solo?
Yes, the north is tourist-friendly. Still, always inform your host or guide about your plans.
